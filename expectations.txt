Evaluate Netflix earnings using the following framework:

1. Consensus expectations (baseline)
   - Near-term (Q1 2026):
     * EPS ≈ $0.76
     * Revenue ≈ $12.1–12.2B
     * YoY growth ≈ 15%+ (revenue and EPS)
   - Full-year 2026:
     * Revenue ≈ $51–52B (~13% growth)
     * EPS ≈ $3.1

   Interpretation:
   - Assume steady, durable growth is priced in.
   - Do not assume hyper-growth expectations.
   - Treat Netflix as a maturing platform.

2. Priority evaluation factors (ranked)

   2.1 Forward guidance (highest priority)
   - Compare forward revenue, EPS, and margin guidance vs consensus.
   - If guidance is weak or below expectations → bearish signal.
   - If guidance is strong → primary driver of upside.
   - Override rule: guidance can outweigh an earnings beat.

   2.2 Advertising performance
   - Evaluate progress toward ~$3B annual ad revenue (high growth expected).
   - Assess YoY growth trajectory and commentary on ad demand.
   - Strong ad momentum → bullish.
   - Weak or slowing ad growth → bearish.

   2.3 Margins and cost structure
   - Analyze operating margin trends and forward margin guidance.
   - Identify impact of content amortization timing.
   - Expected pattern:
     * Near-term margin pressure is acceptable.
     * Second-half recovery is required.
   - Failure to show margin stabilization or recovery → negative signal.

   2.4 Pricing power
   - Evaluate impact of price increases across tiers.
   - Key indicators:
     * Churn levels
     * ARPU growth
   - Strong pricing power (low churn + ARPU growth) → bullish.
   - Signs of demand elasticity → bearish.

   2.5 Growth quality (de-emphasize subscribers)
   - Focus on:
     * Revenue growth
     * Engagement
     * Profitability
   - Subscriber growth is secondary but:
     * Continued international expansion is expected.
     * Weak global growth → negative signal.

3. Expectation vs reality framework

   Bull case:
   - Strong advertising ramp
   - Durable pricing power
   - Stable margins with future expansion
   - ~13% sustained growth profile

   Bear case:
   - Growth deceleration (market saturation)
   - Rising content costs
   - Weak forward guidance
   - Lack of incremental growth drivers

4. Reaction function

   - If:
     * Earnings beat
     * AND strong forward guidance
     * AND clear traction in ads + pricing
     → classify as BULLISH

   - If:
     * Earnings beat
     * BUT weak guidance OR weak business signals
     → classify as BEARISH

   - If mixed signals:
     → classify as NEUTRAL with bias determined by guidance

Output:
- Classification: Bullish / Neutral / Bearish
- Key drivers (2–4 concise points)
- Explicit note on whether guidance confirmed or broke expectations
