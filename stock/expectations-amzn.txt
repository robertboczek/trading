Evaluate Amazon (AMZN) earnings using the following framework:

1. Consensus expectations (baseline)

   - Near-term (Q1 2026):
     * EPS ≈ $1.61–$1.64
     * Revenue ≈ $177B–$178B
     * YoY growth:
       - Revenue ≈ +14%
       - EPS ≈ low-single-digit growth

   - Segment expectations:
     * AWS revenue ≈ ~$36.8B (mid-to-high 20% growth baseline)
     * Advertising ≈ strong (~20%+ growth)
     * E-commerce: steady but lower-margin growth

   Interpretation:
   - Solid double-digit revenue growth is expected.
   - Earnings growth is lagging due to rising costs.
   - A “beat” is expected and largely priced in.

2. Priority evaluation factors (ranked)

   2.1 AWS growth (highest priority KPI)
   - Key expectation:
     * ~25–30%+ YoY growth baseline (with upside to ~30–38%)
   - AWS is the primary profit driver.
   - Re-acceleration → bullish signal.
   - Deceleration → bearish (even if earnings beat).

   2.2 AI monetization vs capex (core tension)
   - Amazon is expected to spend ~$200B+ on AI/data centers.
   - Evaluate:
     * Whether AI demand is translating into revenue (AWS + Bedrock)
     * Management commentary on ROI
   - Strong monetization → bullish.
   - Rising capex without clear returns → bearish. 

   2.3 Forward guidance (critical override)
   - Assess Q2 + full-year outlook:
     * Revenue growth trajectory
     * Operating income range
   - Analysts expect strong revenue guidance (~$188B+ Q2 range).
   - If guidance improves → bullish.
   - If guidance is cautious or “messy” → bearish.

   2.4 Margins and operating income
   - Key focus:
     * AWS margins (~mid-30% expected)
     * Retail profitability (North America + International)
   - Concern:
     * AI + logistics costs may pressure margins
   - Margin expansion → bullish.
   - Margin compression → negative.

   2.5 Advertising + retail execution
   - Advertising expected to grow ~20%+ (high-margin segment).
   - Retail:
     * Stable growth expected
     * Margin improvements vs prior years
   - Strong ad growth → supportive.
   - Retail weakness → secondary drag.

   2.6 Consumer + enterprise demand signals
   - Evaluate:
     * E-commerce demand trends
     * Enterprise cloud demand
   - Resilient demand → bullish.
   - Signs of slowdown → bearish.

3. Expectation vs reality framework

   Bull case:
   - AWS growth re-accelerates (>~30%)
   - AI monetization shows clear traction
   - Guidance improves or exceeds expectations
   - Margins remain stable despite heavy capex

   Bear case:
   - AWS growth slows
   - AI spending outweighs near-term returns
   - Guidance cautious or disappointing
   - Margin pressure increases

4. Priced-in baseline vs surprise scenarios

   Baseline:
   - ~14% revenue growth
   - ~$1.62 EPS
   - Strong AWS + advertising growth
   - Heavy AI investment continues

   Upside surprise:
   - AWS growth above expectations
   - Strong AI-driven revenue acceleration
   - Guidance raised
   - Margin resilience

   Downside surprise:
   - AWS deceleration (key risk)
   - Weak or cautious guidance
   - Margin pressure from capex
   - AI narrative questioned

5. Reaction function

   - If:
     * Earnings beat
     * AND AWS growth strong (≥ high-20% / ~30%+)
     * AND guidance improves
     → classify as BULLISH

   - If:
     * Earnings beat
     * BUT AWS slows OR guidance weakens OR capex concerns rise
     → classify as BEARISH

   - If mixed:
     → classify as NEUTRAL with AWS + guidance as primary tie-breakers

6. Market context (critical overlay)

   - Analysts broadly bullish (~90% Buy ratings).
   - Stock has rallied into earnings → expectations elevated.
   - Options imply ~5–7% move (moderate volatility range).

   - Key dynamic:
     * “Strong growth is expected — AWS + AI execution + guidance drive the stock”

- Classification: Bullish / Neutral / Bearish

  * AWS growth (accelerating vs decelerating)
  * AI monetization vs capex (validated vs concerning)
  * Guidance direction (improving vs cautious)
