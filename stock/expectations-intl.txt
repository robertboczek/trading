Evaluate Intel (INTC) earnings using the following framework:

1. Consensus expectations (baseline)

   - Near-term (Q1 2026):
     * EPS ≈ $0.00 to $0.02 (near breakeven)
     * Revenue ≈ $12.2B–$12.5B
     * YoY growth ≈ flat to slightly negative (~-3%)

   - Company guidance:
     * EPS ≈ breakeven (0.00)
     * Revenue range ≈ $11.7B–$12.7B (below prior expectations)

   Interpretation:
   - Weak near-term performance is expected and largely priced in.
   - Expectations have been revised downward into the print.
   - The quarter is treated as a transition period, not a growth phase.

2. Priority evaluation factors (ranked)

   2.1 Forward guidance (highest priority)
   - Evaluate Q2 and full-year outlook vs consensus.
   - Intel has a history of:
     * Beating the quarter
     * Disappointing on guidance
   - If guidance improves or exceeds expectations → strong bullish signal.
   - If guidance is weak or unchanged → bearish (even on a beat).
   - Override rule: guidance outweighs Q1 results.

   2.2 AI / data center momentum (core narrative)
   - Assess growth in:
     * Data center revenue
     * AI-related CPU demand
   - Investors are positioning Intel as an AI recovery story.
   - Strong AI demand commentary → bullish.
   - Lack of traction vs competitors → bearish.

   2.3 Foundry business progress (strategic pillar)
   - Evaluate:
     * Intel Foundry revenue growth
     * External customer traction
   - Foundry is key to long-term re-rating.
   - Weak adoption or continued losses → negative signal.

   2.4 Margin trajectory and cost structure
   - Analyze:
     * Gross margin trend
     * Impact of restructuring costs
   - Expected pattern:
     * Near-term pressure acceptable
     * Gradual recovery required
   - Continued margin deterioration → bearish.

   2.5 PC market stabilization
   - Assess demand recovery in:
     * Client computing segment (PCs)
   - Stable or improving PC demand → supportive.
   - Continued weakness → drag on overall growth.

3. Expectation vs reality framework

   Bull case:
   - Strong AI-driven demand (data center growth)
   - Guidance raised or materially improved
   - Foundry progress gains credibility
   - Margins show path to recovery

   Bear case:
   - Weak or flat guidance
   - Limited AI traction vs Nvidia/AMD
   - Foundry losses persist without progress
   - Margins remain pressured

4. Priced-in baseline vs surprise scenarios

   Baseline:
   - Flat revenue (~$12B range)
   - Near-zero EPS
   - Modest or cautious outlook

   Upside surprise:
   - Strong guidance (especially Q2 revenue above expectations)
   - Accelerating AI/data center demand
   - Margin improvement signals

   Downside surprise:
   - Weak or declining guidance
   - Slower-than-expected AI ramp
   - Continued margin pressure or execution concerns

5. Reaction function

   - If:
     * Earnings meet/beat expectations
     * AND guidance improves
     * AND AI/data center momentum is strong
     → classify as BULLISH

   - If:
     * Earnings beat
     * BUT guidance weak OR AI traction unclear
     → classify as BEARISH

   - If mixed signals:
     → classify as NEUTRAL with guidance as primary tie-breaker

6. Market context (critical overlay)

   - Stock has rallied significantly into earnings (~70%+ YTD).
   - Expectations for turnaround + AI narrative are elevated.
   - Risk:
     * “Good is not enough” — high bar for upside reaction
     * Expectations may be ahead of fundamentals

- Key drivers (2–4 concise points)
- Explicit assessment of:
  * Guidance direction (improving vs deteriorating)
  * AI/data center momentum (accelerating vs lagging)
  * Margin trajectory (recovering vs pressured)
