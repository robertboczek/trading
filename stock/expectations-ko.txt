   Interpretation:
   - Steady, mid-single-digit growth is expected.
   - KO is treated as a stable, defensive compounder.
   - No acceleration is priced in—execution consistency is the bar.

2. Priority evaluation factors (ranked)

   2.1 Volume vs pricing mix (highest priority)
   - Decompose organic growth into:
     * Pricing (primary driver recently)
     * Volume (weak to flat in recent quarters)
   - Key question:
     * Is growth still pricing-led, or is volume improving?
   - Volume recovery → bullish signal.
   - Continued reliance on pricing → bearish/neutral.

   2.2 Forward guidance
   - Evaluate full-year organic growth and EPS outlook.
   - KO recently guided:
     * ~4–5% organic growth
     * ~7–8% EPS growth
   - If guidance is raised or shows confidence → bullish.
   - If guidance remains cautious or weak → bearish.
   - Override rule: guidance outweighs small earnings beats.

   2.3 Pricing power durability
   - Assess ability to continue raising prices without volume erosion.
   - Monitor:
     * Consumer pushback
     * Elasticity in developed markets
   - Sustained pricing power → bullish.
   - Signs of demand weakening due to pricing → bearish.

   2.4 Margins and cost structure
   - Analyze:
     * EBIT margin (~mid-30% range expected)
     * Input cost pressures (commodities, packaging)
   - Expected:
     * Stable to slightly improving margins
   - Margin compression → negative signal.

   2.5 Consumer demand health (global)
   - Evaluate regional demand:
     * North America and Asia recently weaker
     * Emerging markets mixed
   - Determine:
     * Temporary softness vs structural slowdown
   - Broad demand weakness → bearish.
   - Stable global demand → supportive.

   2.6 Product mix and innovation
   - Monitor growth in:
     * Zero-sugar products
     * Water / premium beverages
   - Investors expect portfolio diversification to support growth.
   - Weak innovation or mix shift → negative.

3. Expectation vs reality framework

   Bull case:
   - Volume growth stabilizes or improves
   - Pricing power remains intact without demand loss
   - Margins remain strong or expand
   - Guidance improves or signals confidence

   Bear case:
   - Growth remains pricing-driven with weak volumes
   - Consumer demand softens (especially in developed markets)
   - Guidance remains cautious or is lowered
   - Margin pressure emerges

4. Priced-in baseline vs surprise scenarios

   Baseline:
   - ~4–5% organic growth
   - ~7–8% EPS growth trajectory
   - Strong pricing, weak/flat volumes
   - Stable margins

   Upside surprise:
   - Clear volume acceleration (not just pricing)
   - Stronger-than-expected organic growth
   - Guidance raised
   - Margin expansion

   Downside surprise:
   - Volume declines persist or worsen
   - Revenue miss driven by demand weakness
   - Guidance disappoints (key risk)
   - Pricing power shows cracks

5. Reaction function

   - If:
     * Earnings meet/beat expectations
     * AND volume trends improve
     * AND guidance is stable or raised
     → classify as BULLISH

   - If:
     * Earnings beat
     * BUT growth is pricing-driven OR guidance weakens
     → classify as BEARISH

   - If mixed:
     → classify as NEUTRAL with volume + guidance as primary tie-breakers

6. Market context (critical overlay)

   - KO is a defensive, dividend-oriented stock:
     * Lower volatility, but sensitive to macro + consumer trends
   - Current concerns:
     * Demand softness in key regions
     * Over-reliance on pricing vs volume
   - Key dynamic:
     * “Quality of growth” (volume vs pricing) matters more than absolute growth
