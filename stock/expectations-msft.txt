Evaluate Microsoft (MSFT) earnings using the following framework:

1. Consensus expectations (baseline)

   - Near-term (Q3 FY2026):
     * EPS ≈ $4.04–$4.07
     * Revenue ≈ $81.3B–$81.5B
     * YoY growth:
       - Revenue ≈ +16%
       - EPS ≈ +16–17%

   - Full-year FY2026:
     * Revenue ≈ ~$324B–$327B
     * EPS ≈ ~$16.5–$17.1

   Interpretation:
   - Strong double-digit growth is expected.
   - Microsoft is priced as a high-quality AI + cloud compounder.
   - A beat is expected and largely priced in.

2. Priority evaluation factors (ranked)

   2.1 Azure growth (highest priority KPI)
   - Key metric:
     * Azure growth ≈ high-30% expected (≈37–39%)
   - This is the most important driver of valuation.
   - Re-acceleration or >~39% growth → bullish signal.
   - Deceleration → bearish (even if earnings beat).

   2.2 AI monetization (core narrative)
   - Evaluate:
     * Copilot adoption (enterprise + consumer)
     * AI contribution to revenue growth
   - Key question:
     * Is AI translating into real revenue or just usage?
   - Strong monetization → bullish.
   - Weak conversion → bearish.

   2.3 Forward guidance (critical override)
   - Assess Q4 + full-year guidance:
     * Revenue growth trajectory
     * Azure outlook
     * Margin expectations
   - If guidance improves → bullish.
   - If guidance is cautious or unchanged → bearish.
   - Override rule: guidance outweighs quarterly beat.

   2.4 AI capex vs returns (key tension)
   - Evaluate:
     * Capital expenditures (data centers, GPUs)
     * Commentary on ROI / utilization
   - Market concern:
     * ~$600B industry-wide AI spend needs justification
   - Controlled spending + clear ROI → bullish.
   - Rising capex without returns → bearish.

   2.5 Margins and operating leverage
   - Expected:
     * Strong margins (~mid-40% operating margin range)
   - Watch for:
     * AI infrastructure diluting margins
   - Stable or expanding margins → bullish.
   - Margin compression → negative.

   2.6 Broader segment performance
   - Assess:
     * Intelligent Cloud (primary driver)
     * Productivity & Business (Office, LinkedIn)
     * More Personal Computing (Windows, Xbox)
   - Weakness outside AI/cloud → secondary drag.

3. Expectation vs reality framework

   Bull case:
   - Azure growth re-accelerates (≈39%+)
   - AI monetization shows clear traction (Copilot growth)
   - Guidance improves or signals confidence
   - Margins remain strong despite capex

   Bear case:
   - Azure growth slows
   - AI revenue contribution unclear
   - Guidance cautious or disappointing
   - Capex rises without visible returns

4. Priced-in baseline vs surprise scenarios

   Baseline:
   - ~16% revenue growth
   - Strong Azure growth (~high-30%)
   - Continued AI investment
   - Stable margins

   Upside surprise:
   - Azure growth above expectations
   - Strong AI monetization (enterprise adoption)
   - Guidance raised
   - Margin resilience despite capex

   Downside surprise:
   - Azure deceleration
   - Weak AI monetization signals
   - Guidance disappoints (key risk)
   - Capex concerns dominate narrative

5. Reaction function

   - If:
     * Earnings beat
     * AND Azure growth strong (≥ high-30%)
     * AND guidance improves
     → classify as BULLISH

   - If:
     * Earnings beat
     * BUT Azure slows OR guidance weakens
     → classify as BEARISH

   - If mixed:
     → classify as NEUTRAL with Azure + guidance as tie-breakers

6. Market context (critical overlay)

   - Microsoft has:
     * Consistently beaten earnings expectations
     * Strong analyst sentiment (majority Buy ratings)
   - However:
     * Stock reaction has recently been driven more by guidance than results
     * AI spending scrutiny is increasing across Big Tech

   - Key dynamic:
     * “Strong growth is expected — differentiation comes from AI execution + guidance”

- Classification: Bullish / Neutral / Bearish
- Key drivers (2–4 concise points)
- Explicit assessment of:
  * Azure growth (accelerating vs decelerating)
  * AI monetization (real vs narrative)
  * Guidance direction (improving vs cautious)
  * Capex vs returns (efficient vs concerning)
