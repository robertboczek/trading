Evaluate Procter & Gamble (PG) earnings using the following framework:

1. Consensus expectations (baseline)

   - Near-term (Q3 FY2026):
     * EPS ≈ $1.56–$1.57
     * Revenue ≈ $20.5B–$20.6B
     * YoY growth:
       - Revenue ≈ +3–5%
       - EPS ≈ +1–2%

   Interpretation:
   - Modest, steady growth is expected.
   - PG is treated as a defensive, mature consumer staples company.
   - Expectations are low-to-moderate; no acceleration is priced in.

2. Priority evaluation factors (ranked)

   2.1 Volume vs pricing mix (highest priority)
   - Decompose growth into:
     * Pricing contribution (~1%+ expected)
     * Volume growth (expected to turn slightly positive after declines)
   - Key question:
     * Is growth driven by real demand (volume) or just pricing?
   - Positive volume recovery → bullish signal.
   - Continued weak/negative volumes → bearish.

   2.2 Forward guidance
   - Evaluate full-year organic sales and EPS outlook.
   - PG has recently:
     * Maintained cautious guidance amid weak demand signals
   - If guidance improves → bullish.
   - If guidance is lowered or cautious → bearish.
   - Override rule: guidance outweighs small earnings beats.

   2.3 Margins and cost pressures
   - Focus on:
     * Gross margin trend (declining for multiple quarters)
     * Input cost pressures (energy, commodities, geopolitics)
   - Expected:
     * Continued pressure near-term
   - Key signal:
     * Stabilization or recovery vs continued decline
   - Ongoing margin compression → bearish.

   2.4 Consumer demand health (macro signal)
   - PG is a proxy for global consumer health.
   - Evaluate:
     * Trade-down behavior (consumers buying cheaper alternatives)
     * Category-level demand (home care, baby, beauty)
   - Weak demand (especially in U.S.) → negative signal.
   - Stabilizing or improving demand → positive.

   2.5 Market share + category performance
   - Monitor share trends in key segments:
     * Fabric & home care
     * Baby / feminine care
     * Beauty / grooming
   - Share losses → bearish.
   - Stable or gaining share → supportive.

3. Expectation vs reality framework

   Bull case:
   - Volume growth turns clearly positive
   - Margins stabilize despite cost pressures
   - Guidance improves or shows confidence
   - Demand proves resilient

   Bear case:
   - Growth remains pricing-driven (no volume recovery)
   - Margins continue to decline
   - Weak or cautious guidance
   - Evidence of consumer weakness / trade-down

4. Priced-in baseline vs surprise scenarios

   Baseline:
   - Modest growth (~3–5%)
   - Slight EPS expansion (~1–2%)
   - Continued margin pressure
   - Early signs of volume stabilization

   Upside surprise:
   - Strong volume rebound (not just pricing)
   - Margin stabilization or improvement
   - Guidance raised
   - Strong category performance

   Downside surprise:
   - Continued volume declines
   - Margin deterioration worse than expected
   - Weak or lowered guidance
   - Broad consumer demand softness

5. Reaction function

   - If:
     * Earnings meet/beat expectations
     * AND volume growth turns positive
     * AND guidance improves or stabilizes
     → classify as BULLISH

   - If:
     * Earnings beat
     * BUT growth is pricing-driven OR margins weaken OR guidance is cautious
     → classify as BEARISH

   - If mixed:
     → classify as NEUTRAL with volume + guidance as primary tie-breakers

6. Market context (critical overlay)

   - PG is viewed as a defensive stock:
     * Lower volatility, but sensitive to macro signals
   - Current concerns:
     * Consumer weakness
     * Rising input costs (energy, supply chain)
   - Key dynamic:
     * “Quality of growth” matters more than absolute growth

Output:
- Classification: Bullish / Neutral / Bearish
- Key drivers (2–4 concise points)
- Explicit assessment of:
  * Volume vs pricing mix (real demand vs inflation-driven growth)
  * Margin trajectory (stabilizing vs declining)
  * Guidance direction (improving vs cautious)
