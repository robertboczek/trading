   - A large beat is partially priced in.

2. Priority evaluation factors (ranked)

   2.1 U.S. commercial growth (highest priority KPI)
   - Core driver of valuation and narrative.
   - Expected: hyper-growth (~90%+ YoY).
   - This reflects adoption of Palantir’s AI Platform (AIP).
   - Acceleration or beat → bullish signal.
   - Deceleration → strong bearish reaction.

   2.2 AI monetization (core narrative)
   - Evaluate:
     * Conversion of AIP demand into revenue
     * Enterprise adoption velocity (“bootcamps”, deal size, new customers)
   - Strong monetization → validates AI thesis → bullish.
   - Weak conversion → narrative risk → bearish.

   2.3 Forward guidance (critical override)
   - Assess:
     * Full-year revenue growth (~60%+ implied baseline)
     * Commercial vs government outlook
   - If guidance is raised → strong bullish signal.
   - If guidance is cautious or unchanged → bearish (given high expectations).
   - Override rule: guidance outweighs earnings beat.

   2.4 Growth durability (sustainability question)
   - Key question:
     * Is ~70%+ growth sustainable or peaking?
   - Investors are testing:
     * Whether current growth is structural or temporary
   - Signs of durability → bullish.
   - Signs of normalization → bearish.

   2.5 Margins and profitability
   - Palantir is now:
     * Profitable with strong free cash flow (~50%+ margin expectations)
   - Evaluate:
     * Operating margin stability
     * Free cash flow conversion
   - Strong margins → bullish.
   - Margin compression → negative.

   2.6 Government vs commercial mix
   - Historical strength: government contracts
   - Current narrative shift: commercial AI growth
   - Investors want:
     * Commercial becoming dominant growth driver
   - Over-reliance on government → negative.

3. Expectation vs reality framework

   - Guidance raised (key driver)
   - Margins remain strong

   Bear case:
