   - Low growth is expected and already priced in.
   - The key question is stability vs deterioration—not growth acceleration.

2. Priority evaluation factors (ranked)

   2.1 Same-store revenue & occupancy (highest priority)
   - Core drivers:
     * Occupancy rates
     * Rental rate growth
   - Recent trend:
     * Flat or slightly declining occupancy and rents (industry-wide pressure)
   - Stabilization → bullish signal.
   - Continued decline → bearish.

   2.2 Pricing power vs demand elasticity
   - Evaluate ability to:
     * Raise rents
     * Maintain tenant retention
   - Key dynamic:
     * Storage demand is highly price-sensitive
   - Strong pricing → bullish.
   - Need to discount to fill units → bearish.

   2.3 Forward guidance (Core FFO outlook)
   - Assess updated FFO guidance vs expectations.
   - PSA has recently issued cautious outlooks.
   - If guidance stabilizes or improves → bullish.
   - If guidance is cut → bearish.
   - Override rule: guidance outweighs quarterly results.

   2.4 External growth (acquisitions / consolidation)
   - Monitor:
     * Acquisition pipeline
     * Integration progress (e.g., National Storage Affiliates deal)
   - Strategic expansion is a key growth lever.
   - Strong execution → positive.
   - Weak or dilutive deals → negative.

   2.5 Cost pressures and margins
   - Key cost drivers:
     * Property taxes
     * Labor / operating expenses
     * Interest rates (REIT sensitivity)
   - Rising costs without pricing offset → margin compression → bearish.

   2.6 Balance sheet & capital allocation
   - Evaluate:
     * Debt levels and refinancing risk
     * Dividend sustainability (core REIT appeal)
   - Stable balance sheet → supportive.
   - Pressure from rates → negative.

3. Expectation vs reality framework

   Bull case:
   - Occupancy stabilizes or improves
   - Rental rates hold steady or grow modestly
   - Guidance stabilizes or improves
   - Acquisition strategy adds growth

   Bear case:
   - Occupancy declines further
   - Rent growth weak or negative
   - Guidance cut again
   - Margin pressure from rising costs

4. Priced-in baseline vs surprise scenarios

   Baseline:
   - Low growth (~2–3%)
   - Stable but soft operating trends
   - Cautious guidance
   - REIT-like steady performance

   Upside surprise:
   - Clear stabilization in occupancy + rents
   - Strong acquisition-driven growth
   - Guidance improved
   - Margin resilience despite cost pressures

   Downside surprise:
   - Continued decline in occupancy or rents
   - Weak guidance (key risk)
   - Higher-than-expected cost pressures
   - Evidence of oversupply in key markets

5. Reaction function

   - If:
     * Results are in line
     * AND same-store trends stabilize
     * AND guidance improves or holds

   - If:
     * Results meet expectations
     * BUT occupancy/rent trends weaken OR guidance declines
     → classify as BEARISH

   - If mixed:
